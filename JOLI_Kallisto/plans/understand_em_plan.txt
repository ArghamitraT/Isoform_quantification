Understanding JK vs LK EM — Step-by-step Study Plan
======================================================
Date: 2026-03-14

Goal
----
Understand both EM implementations (JOLI-Kallisto / JK and lr-Kallisto / LK) from
the ground up, then compare them concretely to identify and fix the divergence.

Step 2: Understand kallisto EM
------------------------------
File: kallisto/src/EMAlgorithm.h

Read in this order:
  1. Constructor (lines 26-48)
       - alpha_  initialized to 1/num_trans (uniform)
       - eff_lens_ computed from REAL transcript lengths
       - weight_map_ computed from eff_lens_

  2. run() — long-read branch (lines 224-357)
       - Main loop: only processes multi-tx ECs
       - Single-tx ECs are SKIPPED during the loop
       - After loop: single-tx counts added ONCE (lines 351-356)
       - Convergence: chcount == 0 and i > min_rounds

  3. compute_rho() (lines 377-399)
       - rho[t] = alpha[t] / eff_len[t]
       - normalize: rho /= sum(rho)
       - TPM = rho * 1e6

Key insight: alpha_ in LK is RAW EXPECTED COUNTS, not probabilities.
Normalization into TPM happens via eff_len division in compute_rho().

Step 3: Understand JOLI EM
--------------------------
File: JOLI_Kallisto/em_algorithm.py

Read in this order:
  1. _preprocess()     — splits ECs into single-tx and multi-tx, builds flat arrays
  2. _em_step()        — vectorized E+M step; single-tx included EVERY round
  3. run()             — loop, convergence, zeroing

File: JOLI_Kallisto/weights.py
  - Phase 1: uniform eff_len = 1.0 (placeholder)
  - Phase 2: real lengths from flens.txt (not yet implemented)

File: JOLI_Kallisto/output_writer.py
  - theta → TPM: rho[t] = theta[t]/eff_len[t], tpm = rho/sum(rho) * 1e6

Key insight: theta in JK is NORMALIZED (sum=1) every round.
Single-tx ECs contribute to every normalization step.

Step 4: Compare — two concrete differences
------------------------------------------

| Aspect                  | LK (kallisto C++)                    | JK (JOLI Python)              |
|-------------------------|--------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Single-tx ECs           | Skipped in loop; added ONCE post-EM  | Included in every EM round    |
| Effective lengths        | Real transcript lengths from index   | Uniform 1.0                   |
| alpha/theta during loop  | Raw expected counts (not normalized) | Normalized probabilities      |

Step 5: Toy example (2 transcripts, 3 ECs)
------------------------------------------
See: debug/toy_em_example.py
Manually trace one round of:
  - LK behavior (skip single-tx in loop, add post-EM, use eff_len)
  - JK behavior (include single-tx in loop, normalize every round, eff_len=1)
Compare the two final TPM vectors to see the magnitude of the difference.
