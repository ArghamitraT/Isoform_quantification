## What We Already Know

**Ground truth structure:**
- Sim1: 71,155 transcripts — Sim2: 78,977 transcripts (different sizes)
- **No zero-TPM transcripts** — every transcript has TPM ≥ 1.008 (simulation floor: every isoform gets at least 1 read). This means there are no true negatives in the GT.
- TPM is very heavy-tailed: median ~2 TPM, max ~15,000 TPM; 75th percentile is only ~6 TPM

**Results summary (LK vs JK MS):**
| Metric | LK sim1 | LK sim2 | JK MS sim1 | JK MS sim2 |
|--------|---------|---------|------------|------------|
| Total transcripts seen | ~77K | ~85K | **218K** | **218K** |
| Spearman (all) | 0.770 | 0.775 | **0.903** | **0.912** |
| Spearman (GT nonzero) | **0.824** | **0.825** | 0.814 | 0.817 |
| RMSE (GT nonzero) | 23.7 | 27.6 | 35.2 | 32.5 |

**Critical open question:** JK MS uses a 218K-transcript index vs GT's 71-79K. The ~140K extra transcripts are zero in both GT and prediction — this artificially inflates "all transcripts" rank correlation. The GT-nonzero metrics actually favor LK, which is the opposite conclusion. Need to understand this discrepancy before trusting any comparison.

**Known bug (to fix after EDA):** `write_results` in `multi_sample_em.py` runs one additional MAP EM round with `alpha_prior > 0`. This resurrects transcripts that were correctly zeroed during training (theta=0 → n=0 → numerator=alpha_prior > 0 → non-zero output). Combined with multi-mapping leakage from the index, this contributes to the 16K false positive non-zero predictions.

### Priority 3 — Inter-sample GT correlation (sim1 vs sim2)

**Goal:** Validate whether the multi-sample shared-alpha assumption is appropriate for this dataset.

**Compute on shared transcripts between sim1 and sim2 GT:**
- Number of shared transcripts, unique to sim1, unique to sim2
- Spearman and Pearson on TPM (linear scale)
- Spearman and Pearson on theta (TPM / sum(TPM)) — this is what the EM actually estimates
- TPM ratio distribution (sim2/sim1) for shared transcripts — shows how much expression shifts

**Interpretation:**
- High correlation → samples are similar → multi-sample shared alpha is a reasonable prior
- Low correlation → samples differ substantially → the JK MS assumption may be violated for this dataset
- This directly validates or challenges the rationale for the multi-sample approach

**Implementation note (for `run_gt_comparison.py`):**

### Priority 5 — Data quality / long-read biases

**Goal:** Understand properties of the simulated reads that affect EM behavior.

**Compute from FASTA files:**
- Total read count per sample
- Read length distribution — PacBio sim reads have characteristic length profile; very short reads map ambiguously

- Multi-mapping rate: fraction of reads in multi-tx ECs vs single-tx ECs
- EC size distribution: how many ECs contain 1 transcript (trivially resolved) vs 2, 5, 10+ transcripts
- Coverage uniformity: do reads cover transcripts uniformly or with 5'/3' bias

### Priority 6 — Leaked transcript analysis (false positive non-zero predictions)

**Goal:** Characterize the 16K transcripts JK MS predicts as non-zero that are not in the GT. Understand whether they are noise-floor artifacts or substantial, and whether multi-mapping or the `write_results` bug is the primary driver.

**Background:** Two known root causes:
1. **Multi-mapping leakage** — non-expressed transcripts share k-mers with expressed ones in the 218K index; EM leaks fractional probability mass to them
2. **`write_results` resurrection bug** — one MAP EM pass with `alpha_prior > 0` revives transcripts zeroed during training (theta=0 → n=0 → numerator=alpha_prior > 0 → survives threshold)

**Compute:**
- Classify all predicted-non-zero transcripts: true positive (in GT) vs leaked (not in GT)
- Predicted TPM distribution for leaked transcripts vs true positives
- Total TPM budget consumed by leaked transcripts (= TPM stolen from true transcripts)
- EC membership of leaked transcripts: what EC sizes do they appear in?
- Comparison with LK: does LK also predict non-zero for the same transcripts (soft leak) or fully zeros them (hard suppression)?

**Figures:**

| Figure | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| `fig_leaked_tpm_histogram.png` | Overlaid log-scale histograms: predicted TPM for true positives (in GT) vs leaked transcripts (not in GT). Key question: are leaked transcripts all near the noise floor, or do some have substantial predicted TPM? |
| `fig_leaked_tpm_cdf.png` | CDF of predicted TPM for leaked transcripts. X-axis = predicted TPM threshold, Y-axis = cumulative fraction of leaked transcripts eliminated. Shows what floor value would eliminate 90%, 99% of leakage. |
| `fig_leaked_rank_plot.png` | All 85K non-zero predicted transcripts sorted by predicted TPM (x = rank, y = TPM, log-scale). Color: red = leaked (not in GT), blue = true positive. Are leaked transcripts only at the bottom of the ranking, or scattered throughout? |

## Open Questions to Answer Through This EDA

1. Are the LK and JK MS experiments using the same reference index? If not, all comparisons are invalid.
2. How correlated are sim1 and sim2 GT thetas? Does the dataset actually support multi-sample learning?
3. What fraction of genes are genuinely hard (balanced multi-isoform)? Is this dataset a challenging test for EM?
4. Does JK MS improve performance on hard genes specifically, or is the "all transcripts" Spearman improvement purely an artifact of the index size?
5. Are leaked transcripts near the noise floor (fixable with a better threshold) or substantial (structural multi-mapping problem)?
6. Is the `write_results` resurrection bug the primary driver of false positives, or is multi-mapping leakage dominant?
